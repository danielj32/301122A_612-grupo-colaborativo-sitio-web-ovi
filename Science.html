Science

The science and technology of Ancient Greece were highly developed compared to other places. Hippocrates emerged in the field of medicine and geometry was developed by Pythagoras and Euclid.

El origen del mundo y los fenómenos meteorológicos, por ejemplo, eran temas que otros pueblos habían explicado por medio de la acción de los dioses. Los griegos pensaron que los hombres, los dioses, el mundo, todo formaba parte de un universo unificado, en el que las cosas se ubicaban en un orden armónico.

And that this order, this cosmos, could be understood by human intelligence. Observing the daily phenomena, the men could understand the origin and the Order of the world. Although they were religious, the Greeks dared to seek answers without necessarily resorting to the mysterious actions of the gods.

The mathematics, which is the basis of all scientific knowledge, was cultivated in a special way by the philosophical school which Acaudillaba Pythagoras. Both in geometry (remember the famous theorem that bears his name and allows to solve the triangles rectangles) corn in arithmetic, the numbers and lines occupied a very important place in their speculations.
