Ancient Greece

Greek mythology explicitly consists of an extensive collection of narratives and implicitly in figurative arts, such as painted ceramics and votive offerings. Greek myths explain the origins of the world and detail the lives and adventures of a wide variety of gods, heroes and other mythological creatures. These stories were originally disseminated in an oral poetic tradition, although nowadays the myths are known mainly thanks to the Greek literature.

Pantheon

Greek mythology placed in Olympus the home of the Olympic gods, the main gods of the Greek pantheon, presided over by Zeus. The Greeks believed that he had built crystal mansions in which the gods dwelt. It is the spiritual analogue of the higher world in shamanic cosmology.

The highest peak is the Mitikas (2917 m), the highest of Greece, and the second the Pico Eskolio (2912 m). Mount Olympus is rich in vegetation, especially endemic.

Gods

In Ancient Greek religion and mythology, the twelve Olympians are the main deities of the Greek pantheon, commonly considered as Zeus, Hera, Poseidon, Demeter, Athena, Apollo, Artemis, Ares, Aphrodite, Hephaestus, Hermes and Hestia or Dionysus. They were called ' Olympic ' because, according to tradition, they lived on Mount Olympus. Although Hades was a great ancient Greek god, and was the brother of the first generation of Olympic athletes (Zeus, Poseidon, Hera, Demeter and Hestia), he resided in the underworld, far from Olympus, and therefore was generally not considered one of the Olympics. In addition to the twelve Olympic athletes, there were many other cult groups of twelve Gods.
